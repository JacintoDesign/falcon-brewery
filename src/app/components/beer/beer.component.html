Red Falcon Ale

An Irish-style red ale, full bodied with a slightly sweet and malty opening, mild hop nose and bitterness, and a long roasted finish. The complex malt profile comes from having five grains, including CaraMunich and Roasted Barley. Winner of numerous beer festival awards.

ABV 5.0% IBU 16

Laggar Falcon Craft Lager

A Bavarian Helles lager, more full-bodied than a pilsner because of the addition of Munich Malt. Crisp and refreshing, with moderate bitterness from Centennial, Cascade and Hallartau hops. A beautiful and easy-drinking beer. Bronze medal winner in the Ontario Brewing Awards for lagers.

ABV 5.0% IBU 42

Bomb Girls Blond Ale

Along with Munition IPA, this beer is Falcon’s tribute to the history of Ajax which was the largest munitions manufacturing site in Canada during WWII, and the home of the “Bomb Girl”. This is a North American style blond ale, light pale gold colour, crisp and spicy taste, and a honey-like aroma.

ABV 4.6% IBU 30

Munition IPA

Munition India Pale Ale has a complex malt nose and taste, very full-bodied, and a citrus hop aroma and flavour that comes from Citra, Columbus and Cascade hops. It is golden in colour, and its smooth taste belies the high IBU of 72.

ABV 6.6% IBU 72

Kent's Best Bitter

An extra special bitter pale ale, rich and full-bodied made from traditional English ingredients, including five types of malt, and East Kent Golding and Fuggles hops. This is an easy drinking pale ale – the original session ale.

ABV 5.6% IBU 40

Salem Stout

An American-style stout, slightly sweeter than a dry Irish stout. Black, opaque colour with a foamy tan head. Full bodied because of the higher alcohol content, rich in mouth feel, with a long finish. Toasty flavour from the Black Malt, and a mild bitterness from Warrior and Cascade hops.

ABV 6.5% IBU 45

Ginger Haze

This ale starts out as a pale ale but has a full 10 kg of fresh ginger added to it. The ginger is not subtle but it is also not overpowering. The beer is well balanced with a crisp malt and slightly citrusy hop background.

ABV 5.6% IBU 35

Light Cruiser English Mild

Mild ale originally meant a young ale and is now more often interpreted as being mildly hopped. The style of beer originated in Britain in the 17th century or earlier, and has a predominantly malty palate. Modern mile ales are mainly dark-coloured with an ABV of 3% to 3.6%, although there are lighter-hued examples as well as stronger examples reaching 6% ABV and higher.

ABV 4.3% IBU 17
